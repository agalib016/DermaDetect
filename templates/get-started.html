High Risk

Melanoma mel

Most Dangerous Malignant

Melanoma is the most serious form of skin cancer, arising from melanocytes — the cells that produce pigment. It can spread to other organs if not detected early, making early diagnosis critical for survival.

Warning Signs

  • Asymmetric moles or irregular borders
  • Multiple or uneven colors (brown, black, red, white)
  • Diameter larger than 6mm (size of a pencil eraser)
  • Evolving: changes in size, shape or color over weeks
⚑ Seek immediate dermatologist consultation if suspected.
High Risk

Actinic Keratoses akiec

Pre-cancerous Bowen's Disease

Actinic keratoses (AK) are rough, scaly patches caused by years of sun exposure. They are considered pre-cancerous because they can develop into squamous cell carcinoma. Bowen's disease is a form of carcinoma in situ (early-stage cancer).

Warning Signs

  • Rough, dry, scaly patch of skin (1–2.5cm)
  • Flat to slightly raised patch, wart-like surface
  • Hard, wartlike surface in some cases
  • Itching, burning or bleeding in the affected area
⚑ Early treatment can prevent progression to cancer.
Generally Manageable

Basal Cell Carcinoma bcc

Most Common Cancer Slow Growing

The most common type of skin cancer globally, BCC originates in basal cells at the base of the outer skin layer. It rarely spreads to other parts of the body, but early treatment is still important as it can grow deep into surrounding tissues.

Warning Signs

  • Pearly or waxy bump on the face, ears, or neck
  • Flat, flesh-colored or brown scar-like lesion
  • Bleeding or scabbing sore that heals and returns
  • Pink growth with raised edges and a crusted center
Low Risk

Benign Keratosis bkl

Non-cancerous Common

Benign keratoses encompass seborrheic keratoses, solar lentigines (age spots), and lichen-planus-like keratoses. These non-cancerous skin growths are very common and often appear as people age. They do not require treatment unless they are bothersome.

Characteristics

  • Waxy, stuck-on, wart-like surface appearance
  • Color ranging from light tan to black
  • Round or oval in shape, vary in size
  • May itch but are generally painless
Low Risk

Melanocytic Nevi nv

Common Moles Benign

Commonly known as moles, melanocytic nevi are benign clusters of pigmented cells that appear as small, dark brown spots. Most people have 10–40 of them. They are usually harmless but should be monitored for any changes using the ABCDE rule.

Characteristics

  • Uniform color — shades of brown, tan or black
  • Symmetrical shape with smooth edges
  • Appear from childhood through early adulthood
  • May fade or disappear with age
Low Risk

Dermatofibroma df

Benign Nodule Harmless

Dermatofibromas are small, firm nodules that form in the skin, most commonly on the lower legs. They are composed of fibrous tissue and are benign. They often result from minor skin trauma such as an insect bite or ingrown hair. Treatment is typically not necessary.

Characteristics

  • Small, hard, raised growth — typically under 1cm
  • Color from tan to dark brown, may change over time
  • "Dimple sign" — dimples inward when pinched
  • Can be itchy or tender to the touch
Low Risk

Vascular Lesion vasc

Blood Vessel Usually Benign

Vascular lesions include cherry angiomas, angiokeratomas, and pyogenic granulomas. They arise from blood vessels in or just below the skin. Most are benign and cosmetic, though some (like pyogenic granulomas) may bleed easily or indicate underlying conditions.

Characteristics

  • Bright red, purple or blue in color
  • Flat or raised — can be tiny or several cm
  • Often appear after age 30, more common with age
  • May bleed if traumatized (pyogenic granuloma)
